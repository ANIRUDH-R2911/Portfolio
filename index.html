About Me

In 2024, I transitioned from the vibrant city of Chennai, India to West Lafayette, USA to pursue a Master’s degree in Autonomy at Purdue University. This move marked my first experience living independently and became a defining year of technical growth, hands-on learning, and personal development.


At Purdue, I have engaged deeply with computer vision, robotics, and intelligent systems through a combination of rigorous coursework and applied projects. My recent work spans multiple layers of the perception stack, including:


  • Reimplementing and extending a video object segmentation pipeline using a dual-stream RGB optical flow framework with SegFormer backbones, slot-attention refinement, and custom feature aggregation to enable stable multi-frame segmentation on the DAVIS-16 benchmark
  • Developing a U-Net based semantic segmentation model to interpret urban driving scenes and reason about vehicles, pedestrians, and traffic signals in autonomous driving contexts
  • Enhancing object detection robustness under adverse weather conditions by integrating environment-adaptive preprocessing into YOLOv8 for reliable perception in challenging visibility conditions

In addition, I worked as a Student Researcher in the College of Engineering, contributing to a faculty-led project on autonomous driving perception. During this role, I developed and evaluated ROS2-based perception pipelines in simulation environments, focusing on system-level behavior, real-time performance, and the interaction between perception modules and downstream decision-making..


Coming from an Electronics and Communication Engineering background at Vellore Institute of Technology, I bring a systems-oriented perspective that bridges embedded systems, machine learning, perception, and control, with an emphasis on building solutions that are both intelligent and deployable.


Beyond academics, I enjoy walking and observing how both robotic systems and urban environments interact with their surroundings. Adapting to life in the Midwest has offered fresh perspectives on how intelligent systems and people sense, adapt, and navigate unfamiliar settings.


Alongside my studies, I work part-time as a student barista at Starbucks. Balancing this role with graduate coursework and technical projects has strengthened my communication, teamwork, and ability to operate under pressure, skills I carry into every collaborative engineering environment I am part of.


Curiosity, adaptability, and a commitment to purposeful design guide how I approach challenges, both technical and personal. I am motivated by building intelligent systems that move beyond controlled demonstrations and perform reliably in the complexity of the real world.

Publications

TITLE : Design of rubble analyzer probe using ML for earthquake


AUTHORS : Abhishek Sebastian; R. Pragna; K. Vishal Vythianathan; Dasaraju Sohan Sai; U. Shiva Sri Hari Al; R. Anirudh; Apurv Choudhary


CONFERENCE : 3rd International Conference on Robotics, Intelligent Automation and Control Technologies (RIACT 2022)


PUBLICATION : AIP Conference Proceedings, 2023 View Publication



Designed an earthquake rubble analyzer probe using TinyML and CNN to detect human presence through sounds like “help,” breathing, and coughing with 89.83% accuracy. The system senses ambient audio inside rubble and monitors real-time environmental parameters such as temperature, humidity, pressure, and air quality. Aimed at improving victim detection and survival assessment in post-earthquake rescue operations.
